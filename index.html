DECLARATION OF PARENTAL RIGHTS AND EDUCATIONAL FREEDOM




To the Department for Education:

I stand firmly in support of the principle that all parents - regardless of background - have the fundamental right to guide the moral, religious, and educational upbringing of their children, free from undue state interference.

If enacted, Clauses 24, 25, and 30 of the Childrenâ€™s Wellbeing and Schools Bill would introduce sweeping powers that are both excessive and deeply intrusive. These measures represent a serious threat to our ability - and our sacred duty - to provide our children with an education rooted in our religious convictions and Torah values.

I believe that the responsibility for a child's education rests first and foremost with their parents, guided by conscience, values, and cultural or religious beliefs. Any law that overrides this right undermines both religious liberty and the sanctity of family life.

As a committed and law-abiding citizen, I deeply respect the institutions and laws of this country. But when legislation threatens the ability of families to raise their children in accordance with their deepest convictions, it calls for principled resistance.

I therefore stand alongside thousands of parents across the community in expressing concern, defending our rights, and upholding the shared values that have shaped Jewish life for generations - values of responsibility, integrity, and freedom of belief.

It is with a heavy heart that I write these words. I ask: how have we reached a point where I feel compelled to say that I cannot, in good conscience, comply with such a law? If this were about personal comfort or convenience, I would remain silent. But this touches something far deeper. It speaks to the very core of who we are - our sense of moral responsibility, our heritage, and our identity as a people. Just as no person can live without air, no community can survive without the freedom to pass on its values and beliefs to the next generation.
